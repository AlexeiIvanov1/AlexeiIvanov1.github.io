Philosophy

My Approach

Business language first. Every initiative I sponsor starts with a board-level question - what risk are we reducing, what capability are we unlocking, what return are we generating - and works backwards into architecture. IT earns its seat at the table by speaking the language of the business, not the other way around.

Security as foundation, not afterthought. I operate from a Zero Trust-first mindset - assume breach, verify continuously, segment ruthlessly. This is not theoretical posturing; it is how I design every environment, from identity governance to endpoint management.

Financial discipline in every IT decision. Technology spend demands the same rigour as any other capital allocation. Every IT investment gets a risk register, a phased ROI model, and clear exit criteria. The CFO should never have to ask what IT is doing - reporting, KPIs, and business cases are built into the operating cadence.

AI with guardrails. Deploy where measurable productivity gains exist, protect data sovereignty, and never let adoption outpace the control framework. AI without governance is a liability. AI with governance is a competitive advantage.

Operating models over project legacies. The measure of a good technology leader is not whether the ERP went live - it is whether the governance, service discipline, and team capability persist long after the project closes.
